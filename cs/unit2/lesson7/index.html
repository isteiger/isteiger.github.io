The Importance of the Census

The Census is probably one of the most important things in our democracy as it counts and gets information on the population which decides many important things.

What does it decide?

US House Seats

The Census decides how many repersentatives we have in the US House.

Emergency Response

First responders and disaster recovery personnel use census data to help identify where and how much help is needed.

Redistricting

State and local officials use Census results to help redraw congressional, state, and local district boundaries.

Tax Dollar Distribution

The Census population count decides how much taxpayer money goes to places, like if the City of Raleigh gets more money than Hillsborough for public services such as police and firefighters.

How do I fill it out?

Age

You must be 18 or older to fill out the Census.

Other Notes

Your household may have gotten it in the mail, but you can only fill one out, the web version or the mail version.
